# Magnifica Humanitas Evaluation Rubric

This rubric translates *Magnifica Humanitas* into actionable, secular criteria for evaluating any tool, project, policy, company program, AI system, research effort, institution, or community initiative.

Source document: [*Magnifica Humanitas*](https://www.vatican.va/content/leo-xiv/en/encyclicals/documents/20260515-magnifica-humanitas.html)

## How to Score

Use this scale for each criterion:

- `0` - Harms, contradicts, or ignores the principle.
- `1` - Names the principle but offers weak or mostly symbolic evidence.
- `2` - Partly satisfies the principle with meaningful but incomplete practices.
- `3` - Strongly satisfies the principle with concrete, accountable evidence.

For a quick read, average scores within each section. For a deeper review, record the evidence behind each score and note unresolved risks.

## Summary Test

Ask: **Is this building Babel or rebuilding Jerusalem?**

- **Babel pattern**: concentration of power, domination, opacity, uniformity, efficiency over dignity, profit over the weak, technical inevitability, and a single imposed "language" of data or control. See [Babel and Jerusalem as civic symbols](https://www.vatican.va/content/leo-xiv/en/encyclicals/documents/20260515-magnifica-humanitas.html#Two_biblical_images), [technocratic power](https://www.vatican.va/content/leo-xiv/en/encyclicals/documents/20260515-magnifica-humanitas.html#paradigm_and_digital_power), and [the culture of power](https://www.vatican.va/content/leo-xiv/en/encyclicals/documents/20260515-magnifica-humanitas.html#The_culture_of_power).
- **Jerusalem pattern**: shared responsibility, dignity, dialogue, justice, truth, subsidiarity, solidarity, care for the vulnerable, and the human person at the center. See [Babel and Jerusalem as civic symbols](https://www.vatican.va/content/leo-xiv/en/encyclicals/documents/20260515-magnifica-humanitas.html#Two_biblical_images), [building for the common good](https://www.vatican.va/content/leo-xiv/en/encyclicals/documents/20260515-magnifica-humanitas.html#Building_), [remaining human](https://www.vatican.va/content/leo-xiv/en/encyclicals/documents/20260515-magnifica-humanitas.html#Remaining_human), and [the construction site of our time](https://www.vatican.va/content/leo-xiv/en/encyclicals/documents/20260515-magnifica-humanitas.html#The_construction).

## Minimum Evidence Checklist

Before assigning a high score, look for concrete evidence in at least these forms:

- A written purpose statement that names the human good being served.
- A map of affected communities, including those most likely to be harmed or excluded.
- A human rights, dignity, or social impact assessment.
- Evidence that affected people participated before major decisions were made.
- Clear accountability owners and appeal or remedy paths.
- Transparency about data, models, optimization goals, limitations, and known risks.
- Worker impact analysis, including automation, surveillance, wages, retraining, and hidden labor.
- Environmental accounting for energy, water, materials, emissions, waste, and infrastructure.
- Abuse and misuse threat modeling, including fraud, manipulation, trafficking, repression, cyberattack, and military use.
- An explicit plan for education, digital literacy, and responsible use.
- A long-term stewardship plan for maintenance, access, portability, repair, and exit.

## Deal-Breaker Flags

These are strong signs that a tool, project, or program is failing the rubric, even if it scores well elsewhere:

- It treats people primarily as data, profiles, targets, users, consumers, risks, or units of productivity.
- It makes high-stakes decisions without meaningful human responsibility, appeal, or remedy.
- It concentrates essential data, infrastructure, or decision power in a few opaque hands.
- It depends on hidden exploitation in labor, resource extraction, moderation, data labeling, or supply chains.
- It claims moral neutrality while embedding contested assumptions in design, ranking, access, or enforcement.
- It weakens truth, trust, democratic deliberation, or the ability to distinguish fact from fiction.
- It exploits attention, vulnerability, loneliness, addiction, fear, outrage, or sexuality.
- It normalizes violence, repression, targeting, escalation, or the removal of human judgment from lethal decisions.
- It promises inevitable progress while ignoring those who are displaced, excluded, surveilled, manipulated, or harmed.
